CONTEXT INFORMATION FOR EMPEROR QUOTES
======================================

1. Marcus Aurelius - "Quod obstat viae fit via."
   Context: 『自省録』で自身に言い聞かせた箴言。障害を活用して前進せよという教え。

2. Marcus Aurelius - "If it is not right, do not do it; if it is not true, do not say it."
   Context: ストア派の倫理観を示し、言行一致を促す忠告。

3. Marcus Aurelius - "Waste no more time arguing about what a good man should be. Be one."
   Context: 理屈より実践を重視し、率先垂範のリーダーシップを説いた一節。

4. Marcus Aurelius - "The happiness of your life depends upon the quality of your thoughts."
   Context: 思考次第で幸福が決まるという内面重視の指針。

5. Marcus Aurelius - "You have power over your mind – not outside events. Realize this, and you will find strength."
   Context: 外的要因ではなく内的反応を制御することが真の強さという教え。

6. Marcus Aurelius - "It is not death that a man should fear, but he should fear never beginning to live."
   Context: 挑戦しないまま時間を浪費することへの戒め。

7. Marcus Aurelius - "Perform every act in life as though it were your last."
   Context: 時間管理と集中力を高めるための自己規律。

8. Marcus Aurelius - "The best revenge is to be unlike him who performed the injustice."
   Context: 徳を保ち、悪に染まらないことを説くリーダーの心得。

9. Marcus Aurelius - "Think of yourself as dead. You have lived your life. Now take what's left and live it properly."
   Context: 死を意識し今を最大限に生きるマインドセットを促す。

10. Marcus Aurelius - "Very little is needed to make a happy life; it is all within yourself, in your way of thinking."
    Context: 質素で内面を重んじるストア的幸福観。

11. Marcus Aurelius - "The universe is change; our life is what our thoughts make it."
    Context: 変化を受け入れ、思考を磨く重要性を説く。

12. Marcus Aurelius - "If you are distressed by anything external, the pain is not due to the thing itself, but to your estimate of it – and this you have the power to revoke at any moment."
    Context: 認知を修正して苦悩を克服するストア派の処方箋。

13. Marcus Aurelius - "Time is a river, the resistless flow of all created things."
    Context: 時間の不可逆性を示し、今を大切に生きることを促す比喩。

14. Augustus - "Festina lente."
    Context: アウグストゥスの座右の銘。迅速さと慎重さのバランスを説く。

15. Augustus - "Whatever is done well enough is done quickly enough."
    Context: 品質を優先し、拙速を戒める指導理念。

16. Augustus - "Young men, hear an old man to whom old men hearkened when he was young."
    Context: 経験に裏打ちされた助言の価値を説く言葉。

17. Augustus - "Have I played my part well? Then applaud as I exit."
    Context: 死の間際、人生を舞台にたとえて発した達成感あふれる言葉。

18. Augustus - "My dear Tiberius, don't let your youth make you so angry if anyone speaks ill of me..."
    Context: 批判への冷静な対処を説いた後継者への助言。

19. Trajan - "Were I still young, I would have crossed over to the Indians, too."
    Context: 晩年にも旺盛な行動意欲を示した遠征談。

20. Trajan - "Anonymous accusations ought not to be admitted in any prosecution..."
    Context: 匿名告発を排し、公正な法秩序を守る司法方針を示す。

21. Hadrian - "Little soul, gentle and wandering..."
    Context: ハドリアヌスが臨終間際に詠んだ自作詩で無常観を表現。

22. Antoninus Pius - "Aequanimitas"
    Context: 臨終の合言葉と伝わる一語で公平沈着な統治姿勢を象徴。

23. Diocletian - "If you could see the cabbages I planted with my own hands at Salona..."
    Context: 退位後、園芸の喜びを語り再即位を拒んだ逸話。

24. Julian - "Whither are we fleeing, my most valiant men?..."
    Context: 戦場で兵士を鼓舞し退却を止めたリーダーシップの逸話。

25. Julian - "Can anyone be proved innocent, if it be enough to have accused him?"
    Context: 推定無罪の精神を先取りし、司法の公正を訴えた言葉。

26. Julian - "Never yield yourself as a slave to your own desires or to the whims of others."
    Context: 自制と独立心を強調するストア的自己規律の教え。

27. Julian - "Do not prefer the flatterer to the friend..."
    Context: 誠実な人間関係とリーダーシップの在り方を説く。

28. Julian - "Who ever found salvation in Alexander's conquests?..."
    Context: 武力より知恵を重んじる哲学者皇帝の価値観を表す。

29. Julian - "If one were to rob human beings of the power to do and say as they please..."
    Context: 市民の自由を尊重すべきだとする自由思想的見解。

30. Julian - "Some people are fond of horses, others of wild animals..."
    Context: 旺盛な読書愛と知的好奇心を示す自己啓発エピソード。
